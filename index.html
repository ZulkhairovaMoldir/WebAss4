Interesting information about countries

Christmas in Ancient Egypt: The Egyptians celebrated the "birth" of the god Horus, who was considered a symbol of resurrection and life. This holiday commemorated the birth of new generations and was associated with agricultural cycles.

Smoking for the Pharaohs: In ancient Egypt, smoking was associated with religious rituals. Findings show that the pharaohs used aromas and smoke to communicate with the gods

The world's first 'floating' hotel: Dubai's Atlantis, The Palm is built on an artificial island and offers a unique underwater experience with views of marine life.

Artificial Islands: The Palm and Seven Digit Islands were created using drainage and artificial fill technology, making them some of Dubai's most famous architectural achievements.

Places where water never freezes: In Georgia, there is Lake Kintrishi, famous for the fact that even in the coldest winter months, its water never freezes, thanks to the hot springs that feed it.

Cave-baked bread: Georgia has a tradition of baking bread in caves. Ovens called tone are used to bake shoti, a traditional Georgian bread that is especially tasty due to the oven's special features.
